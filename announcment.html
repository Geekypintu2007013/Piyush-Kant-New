Dec, 2025

Milestone Achieved

Three-phase electric locomotives in Indian Railways with GTO-based converters were originally equipped with harmonic filters to comply with the signalling interference limits. These filters were retained when these locomotives were upgraded to IGBT-based converters. Frequent failures and fire incidents were reported in these filters during service, and a 40 km/h operational restriction was imposed whenever they were isolated. With over 300 filter failures annually, the issue impacted locomotive availability and train punctuality across the 9,000-unit fleet that forms the backbone of Indian Railways. A system-level evaluation was carried out jointly by Indian Railways and me to assess the necessity of these filters. Converter simulations confirmed compliance of line current harmonics with EN 50238 limits even without the harmonic filter. Field trials with 1,392 test combinations across multiple propulsion makes verified conducted and radiated interference compatibility. Based on these results, permanent elimination of harmonic filters from the three-phase locomotives was authorized, leading to reduced failures, removal of speed restrictions, and projected lifetime savings exceeding INR 50 billion (â‰ˆ USD 560 million). This study provides an important case in railway traction electrification, demonstrating how a critical reliability issue was resolved and brought substantial operational and economic benefits.
